My road to web development

In 2014 I completed my degree in Management Information Systems but couldn't find a company interested in a fresh graduate with little relevant experience. Unemployment left me broke, despondent, and trying really hard to not return to fast food or customer service.

That December, I heard about Dev Bootcamp (now sadly defunct). I applied with a friend, was accepted, and spent the next few months drinking from the proverbial firehose during 80 - 100 hour work weeks.

I've been working as a software and web developer since mid-2015, but if you'd asked me about going that route in 2014, I couldn't have imagined myself capable of doing all of this "mathy" work. I'd been dabbling in HTML and CSS for years, but saw anything more complex than that as wizardry.

That's why I love teaching Women, Wine and Web Design in partnership with Tech Elevator. I've made it my mission to show more women just how creative and interesting this work can be, and that (as a general rule) developers aren't unapproachable supergeniuses, they're just people who learned how to do something new.

Code for the greater good

Here's the thing: we held the first Women, Wine and Web Design on November 9th, 2016. It was the day after the election and boy, it was really hard to come in and try to be optimistic. Seeing so many attendees diving into this work for the first time, though? That lifted my spirits. It still lifts my spirits. I told that group that part of the reason I was there was because at my office, I was the only female developer on a team of ~20 and I really wanted to see that change.

Fast forward to today and there's still a huge gender imbalance in my office, but it is getting better. Women in STEM fields are fighting back against ideas presented in the infamous "Google Manifesto" and working to fix toxic work environments everywhere. Meanwhile, this workshop is being taught by two women who attended last year, went to Tech Elevator, and are now working in the field! So I just want to say thank you for coming out tonight- for being brave (and maybe a little subversive), and for trying something new with us!
